

**AXIS**

O F F I C I A L  R U L E B O O K

*Light • Dark • Elemental Dominion*

2 Players  —  Ages 10+  —  15–25 Minutes

# **Overview**

Axis is a two-player cosmic strategy game played on a 6×6 grid divided into four elemental Zones: Fire, Earth, Air, and Water. Two forces — Light and Dark — inscribe Runes across sacred ground, competing to channel elemental power through the Cruxes that anchor each Zone.

| Objective Seize the Rift — that vast divide between Light and Dark — by dominating the Tug of War track, claim all four Cruxes at once, or outlast your opponent in Flux when the last Rune falls. Victory can come at any moment. Every Rune placed shifts the balance. |
| :---- |

# **Setup**

## **The Board & Zones**

The 6×6 board is divided into four 3×3 elemental Zones. Each Zone is randomly assigned one of the four elements — Fire, Earth, Air, and Water — at the start of each game. No two sessions need look the same.

## **Placing the Cruxes**

Each Zone contains one Crux — a convergence point of raw elemental power. Before play begins, place each Crux on a random cell within its own Zone. Cruxes must obey the Cross Exclusivity:

| Cross Exclusivity No two Cruxes may share the same row or column across the full 6×6 board. If a placement would violate this, reroll until all four Cruxes occupy unique rows and unique columns. |
| :---- |

## **Cards & Starting Hands**

The deck holds 32 cards: 8 each of Fire, Earth, Air, and Water. Shuffle the deck, then reveal 4 cards face-up. To offset the first-player advantage, starting hands are drafted rather than drawn:

* The second player chooses 2 of the 4 face-up cards.

* The first player takes the remaining 2\.

* Discard all 4 original cards from the draft. Draw 2 fresh cards from the deck and place them face-up. This is the common display for the game.

There is no maximum hand size. Whenever a card is taken from the display during play, refill it immediately from the deck. If the deck is ever empty when a draw is needed, shuffle the full discard pile into a new deck and continue.

## **Other Starting Conditions**

| Board | All spaces empty. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **The Rift** | Marker starts at 0 (center). |
| **Crux Control** | All Cruxes start Unbound — controlled by neither side. |
| **Discard Pile** | Empty. Always public knowledge. |

# **Turn Sequence**

On your turn, choose exactly one of the following two actions:

| Action | Name | What You Do |
| :---: | :---- | :---- |
| **1** | **Inscribe a Rune** | Choose an empty space, pay its card cost, and resolve its rewards. |
| **2** | **Draw a Card** | Take one of the two face-up cards from the common display. Refill the display immediately from the deck. |

# **Inscribing a Rune**

To inscribe a Rune, select an empty space and follow these two steps.

## **Paying**

Each space has a set of printed symbols. The total number of symbols is its cost — that many cards must be discarded from your hand. Before paying, you may apply discounts:

* Each friendly Rune already in the same row or column as your target space reduces the cost by 1\. Discounts are optional — you may choose to pay more than the discounted cost.

* Discounts cannot reduce the cost below 0\. A 0-cost space is called Free.

* You may voluntarily pay cards on a Free space to gain activations — paying nothing means gaining nothing. Discounts are optional precisely so this choice remains open.

* You must be able to meet the cost to choose that space.

* You cannot pay with more cards than symbols printed on the space.

* Every card you pay must buy an activation — you cannot dump spare cards that do nothing. A single Affinity card may exceed a small space's symbols only when it is the only way to pay; the surplus is wasted.

| Affinity Cards resonate with the elements. A card counts as 2 — both when paying a cost and as 2 activations — when inscribed on a space in its own element's Zone. This is always active; no control is required. A card is never worth more than 2. Example: a 3-symbol space normally requires 3 cards and yields 3 activations. With 1 resonating card (2\) \+ 1 ordinary card (1\) you cover the cost (2 \+ 1 \= 3\) and gain 3 activations (2 \+ 1 \= 3\) — using only 2 physical cards. You still cannot pay with, or activate, more than the symbols printed on the space. |
| :---- |

## **Activating Rewards**

For each card paid, you may activate one symbol on that space to claim its reward. Choose freely which symbols to use. You may activate the same symbol more than once if it appears multiple times and you pay enough cards.

| Symbol | Name | Effect |
| :---: | ----- | ----- |
| \+ | Flux | Your placed Rune gains \+1 Flux. |
| ▲ | Force | Move the Rift marker 1 space toward your side. |
| ■ | Draw | Take 1 card from the face-up display. |

| Base Charge Every Rune begins with a Base Charge of Flux — **1 by default**. The \+ symbol *adds* to that charge; it does not create it. So at the default charge, even a Rune placed with no activations lands carrying 1 Flux: it counts toward Crux control and the Last Rune tally the instant it is placed. Base Charge is a House Rule — set it to **0** to restore classic Null Runes, Runes that begin at 0 Flux and only gain it through \+. |
| :---- |

| The Cost of Going Cheap The fewer cards you pay, the fewer \+ symbols you activate, and the less Flux your Rune carries above its Base Charge. Discounts are powerful but they come at a price — a deeply discounted space might cost you nothing and reward you with nothing beyond the Rune's base charge. Every placement is a negotiation between what you spend and what you gain. |
| :---- |

# **The Crux**

Each Zone’s Crux is a convergence point — where the elemental lines of its row and column cross. Holding a Crux grants Crux Force and bends the board in your favor.

## **Controlling a Crux**

Crux control is determined by the Runes in the Crux’s row and column across the full 6×6 board.

* **Controlled:** You control a Crux if your total Flux in those two lines is strictly greater than your opponent’s. Only Flux counts — a 0-Flux Null Rune (possible only under the Base Charge 0 House Rule) contributes nothing. At the default Base Charge of 1, every Rune in the lines counts.

* **Unbound:** If Flux totals are tied, or no charged Runes are present in those lines, the Crux is Unbound — controlled by neither side.

Crux control is rechecked immediately after every Rune is inscribed and all rewards resolved.

## **Crux Force**

While you control a Zone’s Crux, every Rune your **opponent** inscribes within that Zone tugs the Rift 1 space toward **your** side — the toll for trespassing on ground you hold. Control is checked the instant the Rune lands, so a Rune that wins your opponent the Crux still pays you the toll on its way in. Your own placements in a Zone you control move nothing on this account; Crux Force is purely a deterrent against intruders. Null and charged Runes alike pay the toll.

# **Victory**

The game ends the instant any condition below is met. Two of the three strike without warning — watch the Rift and the Crux count at all times.

| Condition | How to Win |
| :---- | :---- |
| Rift Break | Push the Rift marker all the way to your terminal end: \+8 for Light, −8 for Dark. Instant win. |
| Fluxmate | Control all four Cruxes simultaneously. Instant win. |
| Last Rune | When the board is fully covered in Runes, tally Flux. Highest total wins. |

## **The Rift**

The Rift begins balanced at 0\. Each Force (▲) symbol activated — plus any Crux Force you collect when an opponent inscribes in a Zone you control — drives the marker one step toward your side. Light pushes toward \+8, Dark toward −8. The moment one side reaches its end, that force tears the Rift open and wins.

The Rift also confers foresight. While the marker sits on your side of the Rift track, you may look at the top card of the deck at any time. When the marker is exactly at 0, neither player has this ability.

## **Last Rune — Counting Flux**

If the board fills with Runes before either instant victory triggers, both sides count their Flux using the two sources below. The higher total claims the world.

| Flux Source | How It’s Counted |
| :---- | :---- |
| Total Flux | Sum of all Flux values on your Runes across the entire board. |
| Zone Aura | \+1 Flux for each Null Rune (0 Flux) inside a Zone whose Crux you control — yours and your opponent’s alike. The Crux itself does not score. **At the default Base Charge of 1 no Rune is ever Null, so Zone Aura only scores under the Base Charge 0 House Rule.** |

# **Quick Reference & Glossary**

## **Inscription Cost at a Glance**

* Base cost \= total \# of symbols printed on the space (1–7).

* Discount: −1 per friendly Rune in the same row or column. Floor: 0\.

* Max cards paid \= number of symbols on the space.

* Affinity: a card counts as 2 (cost \+ activations) on a space in its own element's Zone — always.

## **Crux Control at a Glance**

* Compare total Flux in the Crux’s row \+ column. Strictly greater \= Held. Tied or zero \= Unbound.

* Rechecked immediately after every Rune and all rewards resolve.

* Holding a Crux grants Crux Force — each Rune your opponent inscribes within that Zone moves the Rift \+1 toward you.

## **Victory at a Glance**

* Rift marker reaches ±8 → Rift Break. Instant win.

* All four Cruxes controlled simultaneously → Fluxmate. Instant win.

* Board full → Last Rune. Count Flux (Total \+ Zone Aura). Higher wins.

## **Key Terms**

### **Affinity**

A card’s natural resonance with its element. A card counts as 2 — when paying costs and when activating symbols — on a space in its own element’s Zone. Always active; no control required.

### **Base Charge**

The Flux a Rune starts with the moment it is inscribed, before any \+ activations. **1 by default**, so every Rune is charged on placement. A House Rule may set it to 0, restoring classic Null Runes.

### **Crux**

The convergence point at the heart of each Zone. Controlling it grants Crux Force. Control is determined by the Flux in its row and column.

### **Crux Force**

A deterrent granted while controlling a Crux: each Rune your *opponent* inscribes within that Zone drives the Rift \+1 toward *you*, beyond any ▲ they activate for themselves. Your own placements there move nothing. Control is read the instant the Rune lands, so even the Rune that flips the Crux pays you the toll.

### **Flux**

The power held within a Rune. Every Rune starts with its Base Charge (1 by default) and accumulates more through the \+ symbol; a Rune with 0 Flux is Null (possible only at Base Charge 0). Flux determines Crux control and scores in the Last Rune tally.

### **Fluxmate**

An instant victory: control all four Cruxes at the same moment. It is Flux in the Crux lines — not mere presence — that determines control.

### **Free Space**

A space whose cost, after discounts, is 0\. No cards paid, no symbols activated.

### **Last Rune**

The end-game condition triggered when the board is fully covered. Both sides count Flux; highest total wins.

### **Null Rune**

A Rune with 0 Flux — possible only under the Base Charge 0 House Rule (at the default charge of 1, no Rune is ever Null). It counts toward discounts but not Crux control. At end game, each Null Rune scores \+1 Flux via Zone Aura for whoever controls that Zone — placing one in an opponent’s Zone feeds their score.

### **Rift**

The great divide between Light and Dark, tracked on a −8 to \+8 scale. While the marker is on your side of the Rift track, you may peek at the top card of the deck at any time. Driving the marker to your end is a Rift Break — instant victory.

### **Rift Break**

An instant victory: push the Rift marker all the way to your terminal end.

### **Rune**

A marker inscribed on the board by a player. Runes begin with their Base Charge (1 Flux by default) and gain more through the \+ symbol. A Rune is yours from the moment it is placed.

### **Unbound**

The state of a Crux controlled by neither side — Flux in its lines is tied or absent.

### **Zone**

One of the four 3×3 elemental regions of the board, each anchored by a Crux.

### **Zone Aura**

\+1 Flux for each Null Rune inside a Zone whose Crux you control. This includes your opponent’s Null Runes — any uncharged presence in your Zone scores for you. Because Null Runes exist only at Base Charge 0, Zone Aura is dormant under the default Base Charge of 1.
